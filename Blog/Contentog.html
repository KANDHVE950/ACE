Namit Jain, a student of Civil Engineering from IIT-Guwahati topped the UPSC – ESE (Engineering Services Exam) 2017.


Acing a national level examination. A euphoria which only a few chosen ones get to experience. Cracking an entrance exam surely makes us feel proud of ourselves, but victory tastes the sweetest to the first ranker. Fortunately, an alumnus of our institute, recently achieved this triumph.




There is nothing impossible to him who will try

A 2015 passout from the institute, Namit hails from the city of Shivpuri in Madhya Pradesh. He comes from a modest family. His father works in the engineering sector. He has a younger brother who is studying in an IIT as well. After graduation Namit took up a job in the structural engineering firm Thornton Tomasetti. Working there for ten months got him frustrated as he realized that he was not able to make a difference through his work in this sector. He contemplated about switching to a job in IT sector but was apprehensive of the possibility that it might turn out to be a similar experience as his previous job. His job at the firm almost made him hate civil engineering

But he knew that it was the job that had developed such negative feelings in him and his love for the subject could be realized only if he did some substantial work in that domain. So he called up his father and told him about his wish to become an IES officer, who was delighted to hear the news. At that moment, began Namit’s journey of cracking the Engineering Services Examination.


“In the words of Alexander the Great, There is nothing impossible to him who will try.”

Namit also achieved a close to impossible feat by not only securing a rank but also acing the ESE 2017 within just six months of preparation. He came to Delhi and enrolled in a coaching institute in June 2016. He sates that he greatly relied on the coaching material as it provided him a filtered syllabus with focus on scoring as much as possible in the exam. He referred to standard books only during the interview preparations.Namit stresses on the importance of studying current affairs for the exam and he during his preparation he used to study current affairs every morning before starting his core subject studies for the day.He believes in an integrated approach for preparing for the mains and the prelims rather than studying separately for each.


For The Interview round,

Namit emphasized on the fact that the Detailed Application Form (DAF) which is given before the interview, should be filled very meticulously. It can be instrumental in turning the tide of the interview in the candidate’s favour. Namit kept connected with his friends from college who has previously cracked the exam. He states that it really helped him monitor his growth throughout his preparation.


In addition to the UPSC – ESE, Namit has also cleared various other exams such as GATE, BARC, PGCIL and IOCL.


He plans to join the railway sector after his training.


Namit was a resident of the Manas hostel and was an active participant in various extra-curricular activities, especially those pertaining to music as loves to play the guitar and sing along with it.


We wish him loads of luck for his life as an IES officer.
